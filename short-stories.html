Short stories often capture a single powerful moment in just a few pages, creating lasting impact with careful detail and short storytelling.

The Lottery

Shirley Jackson’s chilling tale reveals how tradition and blind conformity can lead to horrifying outcomes. The suspense builds quietly, making the final twist unforgettable.

The Tell-Tale Heart

Edgar Allan Poe masterfully explores guilt and paranoia as a murderer descends into madness. A tense, psychological story that stays with you long after reading.

A Very Old Man with Enormous Wings

Gabriel García Márquez tells a magical-realist story about an angelic figure who appears in a village, highlighting human curiosity, cruelty, and compassion in a subtle, moving way.

Harrison Bergeron

Kurt Vonnegut’s satirical story explores a dystopian society obsessed with enforced equality. A sharp, thought-provoking narrative about individuality and freedom.

The Yellow Wallpaper

Charlotte Perkins Gilman presents a haunting tale of a woman’s descent into madness, critiquing societal treatment of women and mental health in the 19th century.

The Gift of the Magi

O. Henry’s timeless story of love and sacrifice captures the beauty of giving, illustrating how thoughtful gestures can carry immense emotional value beyond material wealth.
